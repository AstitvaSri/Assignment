4 Objective

ITWS-2 Spring 2017 is an introduction to building web applications. This will require the student to learn concepts of functional and objected-oriented programming and the actor model of communication between programs. The student will gain experience working with several powerful technologies, including editors (Emacs), programming languages (Elisp, Racket, Javascript, Python, SQL and Bash), and associated libraries (JQuery, Flask, etc.) and modern, industrial practices (Version control, Literate Programming, building and deployment, unit testing, etc.).

6.2 Attendance

You are expected to meet the institute requirements for attendance of lectures, labs and exams. Attendance will be taken in the labs and the lectures each time.

6.3 Make up Exams

Unless there is a medical or family emergency, or other similar extenuating circumstances, you will not be given exemption from exams. You can expect make up exams to be significantly more difficult than the regular exams.

6.4 Punctuality

You are expected to be seated in the class before the beginning of the class. Your entry to the lecture or the lab may be denied if you are late.

6.5 Cheating policy

Copying answers during quizzes, homeworks, or exams is considered cheating. If you cheat during an exam, you will get a zero for that exam. If you cheat by copying someone else's lab/homework or letting someone copy your homework, you will receive a zero in all your homeworks.
